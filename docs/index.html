How this view works

Every company is scored 0–100: momentum (40% — relative strength vs SMH, distance from 52-week high, trend), growth (35% — revenue growth, earnings growth, gross margin), and value-for-growth (25% — forward P/E against growth plus analyst target upside). Layer heat blends the layer's median relative strength with its breadth. The catch-up radar encodes the trade most people miss: when a layer turns hot, the names that haven't moved yet — but whose growth and trend are intact and whose multiple is not the richest of their peers — are where the rotation tends to go next. That was the Micron setup: AI demand had clearly reached memory before MU's multiple did. Data refreshes daily with the scan; KRX names quote in ₩. None of this is advice — it's a structured map for your own theses.

Free attention signals. The chain pulse strip tracks TSMC and Foxconn monthly revenue from Taiwan-exchange open data — the chain's best free leading indicator of real demand. Each layer also carries Search × (Google Trends interest vs its 12-month norm) and News × (GDELT global news volume vs baseline) chips — attention often moves before price. Hovering a US ticker shows its recent SEC filing activity (insider Form 4 count, last 8-K). These sources are free and occasionally flaky; chips simply disappear when a source is unavailable.

What is this?

SCOUT is an automated research engine. Every weekday after the US close it scans US, ASX, and crypto markets for technical setups (12 pattern detectors: cup-and-handle, breakout, bull flag, double bottom, golden cross, volatility squeeze, pullback-to-trend, and more) and fundamental strength (a Buffett-style screen of quality, moat, value, and management). Each lead gets a 0–100 conviction score, an indicator snapshot, and an ATR-based trade plan. Crypto prices stream live from Binance; equity quotes refresh every 30 minutes during US market hours. It never trades — every lead is a starting point for your own research, not advice.

How conviction is scored

The technical score blends four sub-scores: setup (the strongest chart pattern, with smaller credit for confirming patterns), trend (moving-average structure and ADX), momentum (RSI, MACD, 1-month rate of change), and volume (recent volume versus baseline and on-balance-volume slope). The fundamental score blends quality (ROE, margins, debt, free cash flow), moat, value (P/E), and management (buybacks). A name that is strong on both fronts gets an alignment bonus and the High Conviction tier. The radar lists names that nearly cleared the bar.

Trade plans

Each lead carries levels for framing — not orders. Entry is the last close. Stop is the tighter of the 20-day swing low and 2×ATR below entry, never closer than 1×ATR. Target 1 is 2×ATR above entry; Target 2 uses the pattern's measured move (cup depth or flag pole) when one fired, else 4×ATR. R:R is reward to Target 1 over risk to the stop.

Track record & backtest

The track record panel scores every past lead from the day it first surfaced: forward returns at 1 week / 1 month / 3 months, and whether price reached Target 1 before the stop. It updates daily and does not forget the losers. The detector edge panel comes from a walk-forward backtest (8 years of US data, weekly evaluation, ~23,000 points): each pattern's 21-day win rate and average return next to the universe baseline — a pattern only has edge if it beats the baseline. Survivorship bias applies and costs aren't modelled, so treat both as honest gauges, not guarantees. Leads with earnings inside 21 days carry a warning chip — a chart setup means little against a binary event.

Data sources & limitations

Daily history and fundamentals come from Yahoo Finance (free tier — strongest for US large caps, price-only for crypto). Live crypto prices stream from Binance's public WebSocket; the Fear & Greed index comes from alternative.me; intraday equity quotes are refreshed by a scheduled job and can be delayed. The engine sees numbers only — no news, no earnings calls. Treat every lead as a prompt to dig deeper, never as gospel.

Glossary
RSI
0–100 momentum gauge. ~50 neutral; 50–70 healthy; 70+ overbought (caution).
MACD
Trend-momentum crossover of two exponential averages; a fresh bullish cross is an early turn signal.
ADX
Trend strength, direction-agnostic. Above 25 = trending; below 20 = chop.
ATR
Average daily trading range — the noise band. Stops and targets are sized in ATRs.
OBV
On-balance volume. Rising OBV ahead of price suggests quiet accumulation.
Bollinger squeeze
Volatility bands at their tightest in months — energy stored for a move.
Golden cross
50-day average crossing above the 200-day; a classic regime change.
Cup & handle
A rounded base, a shallow pullback, price pressing the prior high: breakout-ready.
Quality / Moat / Value
Buffett-style reads: how good the business is, how defensible its profits are, and whether the price is reasonable.
Backing
Institutional + insider ownership — whether the smart money is already in.
